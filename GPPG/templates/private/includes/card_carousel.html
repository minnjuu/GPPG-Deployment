Other pangolins

Ground Pangolin

Known as: Temminck's Pangolin, Cape Pangolin

Scientific Name: Smutsia temminckii

Location: Southern and Eastern Africa

Unique Feature: Tough, overlapping scales provide armor-like protection

Diet: Ants and termites

Behavior: Solitary and nocturnal (active at night), Excellent burrowers often use abandoned aardvark or warthog burrows

Status: Vulnerable

Chinese Pangolin

Known as: Chinese Scaly Anteater

Scientific Name: Manis pentadactylai

Location: Northern Indian subcontinent, northern parts of Southeast Asia and southern China.

Unique Feature: Covered in protective keratin scales.

Diet: Ants and termites

Behavior: Solitary and nocturnal (active at night), Spends most of its time on the ground.

Status: Critically Endangered

Black-Bellied Pangolin

Known as: African black-bellied pangolin or ipi

Scientific Name: Phataginus tetradactyla

Location: Southern and Eastern Africa

Size: Smallest of the eight extant pangolin species, with a body length of 30–40 cm (12–16 in) and a tail even longer, reaching 60–70 cm (24–28 in).

Unique Feature: Has the record for the most vertebrae (46-47) in a mammal's tail!

Diet: Unlike other pangolins, they primarily feed on ants instead of termites.

Behavior: Diurnal (active during the day) and spends most of its time in trees.

Status: Vulnerable

White Bellied Pangolin

Known as: The tree pangolin

Scientific Name: Phataginus tricuspis

Location: Equatorial Africa, ranging from Guinea through Sierra Leone and West Africa to Central Africa.

Unique Feature: Strong, prehensile tail that helps them climb trees.

Diet: Primarily insectivores, feeding on ants and termites using their long, sticky tongues.s

Behavior: Solitary and nocturnal. Can walk on all fours or bipedally using their tail for balance.

Status: Endangered

Sunda Pangolin

Known as: Malayan or Javan Pangolin

Scientific Name: Manis javanicai

Location: Throughout Southeast Asia (Thailand, Myanmar, Malaysia, Singapore, Vietnam, Indonesia, and various islands)

Unique Feature: Can be more arboreal than other pangolins, spending time in trees

Diet: Ants and termites

Behavior: Can walk on all fours or bipedally, using their tail for balance

Status: Critically Endangered

Giant Pangolin

Known as: Giant Pangolin

Scientific Name: Smutsia gigantea

Location: Central and West Africa

Unique Feature: Largest of all pangolin species, can reach up to 1.8 meters (5 ft 11 in) in length, Primarily terrestrial (ground dwelling)

Diet: Ants and termites

Behavior: Solitary and nocturnal (active at night) Strong diggers, build their own burrows

Status: Endangered

Indian Pangolin

Known as: Thick-tailed Pangolin

Scientific Name: Manis crassicaudata

Location: Found throughout India (except the arid regions and high Himalayas) and some parts of Bangladesh, Nepal, Pakistan, and Sri Lanka.

Unique Feature: Has the largest scales compared to other Asian pangolins and a thick tail.

Diet: Primarily insectivorous, feeding heavily on ants and termites with their long, sticky tongues.

Behavior: Primarily terrestrial (ground dwelling) but can climb short distances. Also solitary and shy.

Status: Endangered
